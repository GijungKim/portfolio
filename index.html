Overview - IDEO Design Process

One of our assignments in CS3240 Interaction Design at the National University of Singapore asked to describe how we perceive the IDEO Design Process in the shopping cart video. My take revolved around a spinoff of alphabet soup.

- You can take the letters and form any word just like how the IDEO team can take something and redesign it. - The soup taste is catered for the consumer just like how IDEO innovates from the consumer perspective.

- There are different letters which represents the different set of skills within the IDEO team. For example in the shopping cart video, the team consists of an engineer, MBA, linguist, marketing, psychologist, and a biology major

- Not one letter is more important than the other similar to how there is no distinct hierarchy of ranking at IDEO. There is value with each letter as well as each member of the team.

“Design thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that draws from the designer's toolkit to integrate the needs of people, the possibilities of technology, and the requirements for business success.”

— Tim Brown, CEO of IDEO

Overview - Asia Travel Vlogs

Travel vlogs that I started to create once I came to Singapore for exchange. With a help of a few friends and my tripod, I created 1 minute vlogs of each country in Asia that I visited. There is notable progression of my skill in filming as well as video editing from my very first vlog to my most recent vlog. This was all recorded on the GoPro Hero Black 5.

Duration

September 2018 - November 2018

Design Process

Prior to September 2018, I have never touched video editing software, much less film videos. Upon coming to Singapore for exchange, I decided to pursue a new hobby as I began to travel around Asia in the 5 months that I would be studying in Singapore. Beginning with my investment of a GoPro Hero Black 5, I needed to understand how the piece of technology worked to reach my potential in videography. Diving into more indepth features of the GoPro, I realized that I was also understanding more of the GoPro IxD that the designer of the GoPro intended. The entire design process of creating a vlog that excited and also troubled me is that it is truly an iterative process. The iterative repetition of choosing, trimming, editing, and shifting the traveling clips to perfect the transition as well as the rhythm matching of the background music is no easy feat. Ten seconds of vlog footage could take thirty minutes and up to three hours to finalize. If I were to break my design process of my vlogs into steps, they would be:

1. Film anything and everything that may have the slightest potential.

2. Film the same things at different angles to maximize footage potential.

3. Look through all the clips of a trip and select the clips that capture the moment well.

4. Figure out the mood you want the users to experience while watching the vlog.

5. Have a few song choices that you believe fit the mood.

6. Experiment with the clips and the selected song until you find the best composition.

7. Get feedback from peers to see if there is anything to change, remove, or improve.

8. Based on feedback, I generally go back to step 5 or 6.

9. Happy with the vlog? Go to step 10.

10. Share the vlog with your peers!

Overview -Bostony

Bostony is a photography website made with Bootstrap, HTML5, and CSS3. Bostony aims to inspire travelers and photographers alike to discover scenic shots in each respective city. Users will navigate the website to see a photo with the location of where the photograph was taken via Google Maps.

- Presented demo to Boston University CS103 Professor Vahid

Duration

January 2017 - May 2017

Motivation

Photography is a hobby that holds dear to me, not because of how aesthetically pleasing a photo can be, but because of the memories behind each photo. I believe photography can capture the moments of bliss that we all experience when go about our day. To stir on that same belief, I created Bostony to inspire and aid others in capturing the scenic shots that have kept me in awe at the moment.

Design Process

The Design Thinking Process

As this was my first time developing a website, there were many ideas of mine that were simply not feasible with my level of expertise. I knew I wanted to incorporate the locations of the photos that I have taken in a pleasing manner to my target users, but I also needed to emphatize with the beginner photographer up to someone who may be a bit more advanced. A simple place to start would be a photo with the location on Google Maps. Then, I needed to define what the user needs of my website could be. A typical user need would be the user would look for a specific city that they wish to explore and find all the photos that have been taken in that city on Bostony. My ideation of this task at hand started at the foundation of a website. The notorious writer's block of website development however struck me in the beginning.

I went through numerous prototypes with great feedback from my professor and peers on how I might improve the user experience of the website. With ideas such as creating an interactive map, I had to look for alternatives due to my lack of experience. Experimenting with image placement on the text as well as how the media should be presented, I decided to go with images on the left side with a map on the right side. I desired for a good level of interaction design, so I adhered to Shneiderman's Golden Rules for the design principles. Following the Color Theory, I carefully chose the color and photos that would be displayed throughout my website.

Evaluation

One way I decided to go about evaluating Bostony was through Nielsen's Heuristics. When I would discover a principle that Bostony violated, I went back to modifying the prototype to satiate the requirement. Although they do not follow all the principles due to the use cases of Bostony, it retains H-1, H-2, H-3, H-4, H-6, H-7, and H-8 of Nielsen's Heuristics. By the end of my evaluation, I presented the prototype below to my professor as the final project of CS103 at Boston University.
